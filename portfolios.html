Week 8: Festival of Economics

I recently attended Festival of Economics event named "What's Next for Central Banks". The two speakers who gave the talk were Dr Jagit Chadha (Director of the National Institute of Economic and Social Research) and Huw Phill (Chief Economist, Bank of England). Given the highly uncertain macroeconomic environment right now, the talk was majorly focused around inflation and the central bank interest rate hike. Dr Chadha had a critical view of BOE's policy saying that it was late with the hike decision whereas Huw Phill stressed that Bank was doing everything in its power to bring back inflation to normal levels. In reality, if we analyse the above two graphs, it can be observed that depite BOE's aggresive rate hike policy it has failed to tame the inflation and with energy prices being sticky, thus preventing BOE from controlling the inflation there is no doubt that rate hikes will continue in the future. However, I agree with Huw Phill's view that because rate hikes have lag effects it will take significant time to return back to 2% level.
